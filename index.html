GLOBAL SECURITY COOPERATION

Established in 1963 under a multinational security directive, the Global Security Cooperation (GSC) operates as a covert intergovernmental task structure responsible for the identification, containment, and analysis of anomalous phenomena posing potential risk to civil stability.

GSC operates through decentralized regional divisions, specialized research facilities, and rapid containment units. Personnel are recruited from military, scientific, intelligence, and medical backgrounds under strict non-disclosure agreements and tiered clearance protocols.

Operational priorities include:

  • Detection and classification of anomalous entities and events
  • Containment and environmental stabilization
  • Controlled research under ethical oversight framework
  • Information suppression and public narrative management when required

The organization does not operate within public jurisdiction and reports through secured international oversight channels. All materials within this network are restricted to personnel with verified clearance levels.

Internal System Notice: Unauthorized access is monitored and archived.
